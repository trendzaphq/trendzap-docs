# Vision

The internet runs on attention. Attention creates engagement. Engagement creates value. But there is no market for it.

TrendZap is the missing market.

---

## The Inefficiency We're Fixing

Every day, billions of interactions on social media platforms create and destroy value in real time. A tweet that goes viral can launch a brand, topple a narrative, or shift a news cycle. A campaign that flops quietly buries its budget. A creator's post either breaks out or disappears into the feed.

The people who work in and around social media — creators, marketers, analysts, traders — all make constant, implicit predictions about what will happen. They act on those predictions. But there is no mechanism to make those predictions explicit, to price them, or to reward accuracy.

Prediction markets exist for elections, sports, financial events. Not for this. Not for the thing that most of the internet's attention economy actually runs on.

---

## What TrendZap Is

TrendZap is a prediction protocol for social media engagement metrics.

Anyone can create a market asking: will this post reach a specific engagement threshold? Anyone with an opinion can take a side. The outcome is determined by a tamper-proof oracle reading official platform APIs. Settlement is automatic and on-chain.

It is a simple product built on a clear observation: people already predict virality constantly. Now they can do it with skin in the game.

---

## The Longer View

We are building toward a world where viral moments are genuinely priced — where the implied probability of a post going viral is a live, liquid signal that reflects the collective intelligence of everyone watching the internet.

That's not a vision for tomorrow. It's a direction that we're building toward with each market created, each bet placed, and each accurate oracle resolution.

The near-term win is a protocol that works reliably, a community that trusts it, and an oracle that is never wrong about what actually happened on the platforms it tracks.

That foundation is what everything else is built on.

---

## Why Arbitrum

Prediction markets need cheap, fast transactions to function. A 24-hour tweet market might have dozens of bets from small stakers. Each bet needs to be economically viable. Arbitrum makes that possible.

More practically: the Arbitrum ecosystem backed us early, and that backing is meaningful. Building with the support of an established DeFi ecosystem — with Chainlink oracle infrastructure, deep USDC liquidity, and an active developer community — means we're not building in isolation.

We chose depth over breadth. One chain, done well.

---

## Built in Public

Everything TrendZap builds is open source under MIT. The contracts, the oracle, the SDK, the subgraph. Not because it's required — because it's the right way to build a trust-sensitive protocol.

Open source means anyone can verify our claims. It means the protocol can outlive any particular team. It means the community can fork and build if we're ever wrong about what users need.

We build in public because we believe the internet deserves financial infrastructure it can actually inspect.
